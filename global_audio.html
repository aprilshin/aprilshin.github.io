Samsung Global Audio Project

Counseled Samsung’s business strategy through
analysis of market trends and user experience feedbacks

Type Client-based Project
Role UX Consultant
Duration Summer 2014

Overview

In summer 2014, I worked as an UX consultant at Wise-UX Global Consulting Group and worked on Samsung Global Audio project. Samsung brought us one of their core business problems and wanted us to address and solve the issues from UX perspectives. Our global team was consisted of 6 other consultants, each of them were bilingual in different languages. As a bilingual in English, I took charge of the U.K. and U.S. markets.

My primary roles are:

1. Counseled partners in strategy meetings through analysis of market trends and user experience feedbacks, highlighting potential product development and untapped demand of audio market in the U.S. and U.K.

2. Developed individualized strategy proposals for Samsung Global Audio through creating up-to-date databases of high-end users, reviewing firm’s marketing process and potential promotion issues in both domestic and global markets.

Problem Define

Initial problem that Samsung brought us was:


"... We ultimately want to make an entry into the high-end & low-end audio market. Therefore, through tailored user study as well as future trends analysis, we want to get help in making our best business decision before jumping into the market..."


After, we re-defined the problem more specifically by asking questions like:


ㆍ How can we define “high-end” and “low-end” audio market?
ㆍ What are the products included in “global audio market”?
ㆍ What are the short-term and long-term business goals?
ㆍ Are there any specific regions to focus?


By digging deeper into questions, we were able to clearly define the problem.

Process

Due to confidentiality, I am not able to reveal the materials I created during the project. But as a quick summary about the process, I was able to experience both conducting very tailored UX study and business strategy/marketing analysis. It was a valuable experiences for me to spend enormous times and efforts in segmenting user types, screening and recruiting users as well as utilizing diverse business strategy methods in the process.

Outcomes

We successfully ended the project, and later this project laid the groundwork for Samsung’s M&A (Merger and Acquisition) of Harman International Industries, Inc., one of the biggest audio, electronic, and infotainment systems companies in the world.

Some of the key features from recently-released audio products of Harman, such as smart speaker or new premium sound bars, were things we included in our final solutions.

Final thoughts

Although Samsung has been regarded as one of the most innovative companies in the world, they are somewhat having a hard time in applying user-centered business strategy. Through this project, I was able to learn how to incorporate both UX process and strategic business analysis into making core business decisions.

As a person who has experienced several business sectors, now I am confident to say that every strategic business decisions cannot be freed from UX feedbacks. By looking at how Samsung is making a huge growth in the audio industry, I also feel responsible to become a UX professional who can provide both insightful and strategic guidance to every problem solving.
