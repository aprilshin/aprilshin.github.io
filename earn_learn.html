Earn & Learn

A skill-sharing and peer-tutoring platform
that connects students based on individual learning need

Type Individual Project
Role UX Designer
Tools Sketch, Balsamiq, Protopie, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Premiere
Timespan Fall 2018 (currently in process) | SI 582: Intro to Interaction Design
Service Slogan Share your skills, make some money, and Go Blue!

The Challenge

The University of Michigan (UM) is a reputable, well-rounded public university with over 50,000 students and faculty members. One day, when I was having difficulties figuring out how to solve my website coding issues, I thought,

‘Since we have so many intelligent students from such a wide range of disciplines, what if I could have a peer helping me out on the specific problems that I’m having?’, and then ‘What if UM students could exchange knowledge and skills according to their learning needs?’

I decided to take this challenge for my individual project in class SI 582: Introduction to Interaction Design, taught by Professor McCarley. In fact, I realized that many UM students had been raising similar questions when they were unable to get help from school resources or could not find a right person to work on their problems with them.

The Solution

Earn & Learn is an application that connects UM students based on individual needs for learning. Learning subjects can be anything from courseworks to technical skills, and students can be a tutor or tutee.

Why this solution?

1) Effectiveness of peer tutoring
According to various research, peer tutoring has its great benefits in higher learning achievement, improved relationships with peers, improved personal and social development as well as increased motivation.

2) Effectivess of in-person tutoring
Many studies show that in-person human tutors can be highly effective in learning. My research also shows that the majority of students found that in-person tutoring is more helpful than online education.

3) Fostering equality in access to in-person learning opportunities
Regardless of its effectiveness, private tutoring is expensive that many students cannot afford. By using this solution, students can have more equal opportunities in finding qualified peer tutor at affordable prices.

4) Providing meaningful economic activity for students
Tutors on the platform (anyone) can have great opportunities to gain valuable teaching experiences as well as building their own brand and business by sharing their skills.

Key Screens & Features







1. Onboarding & Login


Clean onboarding screens allow users to get a quick sense of what the application does.

Various login options including login with social accounts and personal email offer efficient and convenient login experience.







2. Explore & Save


Home screen allows users to explore diverse tutoring sessions on campus.

Users can save classes, which they are interested in and always can go to Saved tap to see their wishlists.







3. Explore in detail


Once users find interesting classes, they can go further explore more.

Users can quickly look at details about the tutor, class, curriculum, and reviews. Once they like the class, they can either save it to the Saved tap, or book the class immediately.






4. Book classes

Users can easily book the class to schedule their first meeting with the tutor.

Users select when and where to meet based on previous input from tutors. Booking class will be immediately notified by tutors and tutors can review tutee's request and directly message them to confirm the first meeting.






5. Switch to tutor mode

Users always can be both tutor and tutee. They can switch to tutor mode by going into Setting tap.

In tutor mode, users can manage and register classes easily and conveniently.

Exploring the solution space

In this stage, I conducted the following two methods: brainstorming and ideation through sketch. In class, we had training about rapid brainstorming with structure with the group. We choose two axes for our design matrix and brainstormed based on the given instructions. This helped me a lot in expanding my ideas and developing creativity.

After the brainstorming session, I completed ideation through sketch: I sketched eight possible solutions to my design problems, which allowed me to think in different contexts where my system could be used to inspire new, creative ideas.

After, I sketched user storyboard to help convey our design problem and solution. By doing so, I was better able to sympathize with my hypothetical user, and reconsider whether my solution really will solve the user's problem.

Finding a good solution

In this stage, I narrowed my scope down to focus on mobile application service. This is because 1) my target users, mostly students, enjoy mobile application service, and 2) it is the most feasible solution in the near future.

I created three personas and one anti-persona to better understand needs and goals of my users. After, I picked my two primary personas and condcuted story mapping for a better product development.

By using all previous efforts, I came up with three design ideas for the solution. I used Balsamiq for a quick wireframing of these three ideas. To evaluate such ideas, I conducted QOC analysis (Questions, Options, and Criteria) to describe my design rationale.

Low Fidelity Prototyping

After thorough discussion with class and professor, I picked the best idea for paper prototyping.

I tested this prototype by assigning users various tasks to complete. When the users got stuck, I asked them to write them down immediately so I could better understand where the breakdowns in the process were happening.

User Feedbacks from paper prototyping:

1. Allowing me to use social accounts to login: Users want to have more flexibility in login experience.

2. I want to know about popular tutors in addition to popular classes: Some mentioned that they would like to see popular tutors as well.

3. Tell me what happens when I book the class: Users want to clearly know what happens when they book the class before making their decisions.

4. Provide me clear information on verification for tutors: Trust and safety always matter. Users want to make sure and see that tutors were verified and trustworthy.

5. Let tutors have right to initiate direct communication with potential tutees: Potential tutors were concerned if users just contact them without serious interest. Also, they were concerned if other users can see too much information about them.
