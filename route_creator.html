Route Creator

An application that allows you
to make your own routes in the most convenient way.

Type Individual Project
Role UX/UI Designer
Duration Summer 2018
Service Slogan Less than a minute. Create your own routes. It’s super quick, flexible, and easy.

Overview

As an enthusiastic traveler, making itinerary takes up a lot of times and efforts. Of course, Google Map is my savior in this, but still, I need extra papers to write down notes to manage my trip. Whenever there comes a few changes in my plan, I should go back to map, search places again, and compare distances to find the most efficient routes for my entire schedule.

I was given this project when taking full-time UX school class at Fast Campus. Fast Campus UX School is a competitive UX professional program in Korea like General Assembly in the U.S. Through design process, I immediately noticed there are many users experiencing same issues as I had. More importantly, there is no satisfactory application that helps users create their own travel routes despite of great amount of needs. 

Based on tailored research with keen insights, I finally designed the application called, Route Creator. The service was nominated 3rd place among 23 others in in-class final design competition.

Goals

Route Creator aims to help users save their times and efforts when they want to:


1) quickly make their own and unique travel plans ahead
2) flexibly adjust or change their trip schedules while traveling
3) easily find the most efficient routes for their everyday life

Approach

I utilized the double-diamond tactic for this project: Discover, Define, Develop, and Deliver.

In Discover phase, the project was conducted as a team, who were also working with the same topic, travel. We conducted quantitative and qualitative research together including survey, individual in-depth interviews. Before delving into next phase, I used affinity diagram to organize ideas and data to draw key findings.

In Define phase, I defined purpose of design: Who, What, How, by identifying target user & problems through user segmentation, persona scenario, and journey map.

In Develop phase, I created blueprint to solve my users’ problems by incorporating creative ideation, UX strategy. Based on UX strategy, I started developing design concept by utilizing mind map, concept keyword, mood board.

In Deliver phase, I created wireframes and had low-fi wireframe session, which provided me precious feedbacks from users. Finally, I finalized my final design and completed prototyping.

Process: Discover

In Discover phase, tailored research is the key. As a team-based research, we conducted desk research by assigning each other different focus: domestic trend, global trend, current popular travel app services. 

After the discussion with the outcomes, we conducted survey with 100+ participants, and individual interviews to figure out current users' experiences with other travel apps and problems they encountered. We later, selected 4 interviewees, who gave insightful responses for in-depth individual interviews.

Key Findings

Process: Define

To me, define phase, is the most important part taking longer times and efforts than any other phases. Defining WHO (target user), WHAT (service blueprint), HOW (method) is the heart of every problem-solving.

As for the first step, I asked myself, “who is my target user?” to identify my core users. In this process, I grabbed 5 sticky notes and define 5 types of people who may need my service the most. Based on this, I drew user segmentation matrix based on two most important factors, age and traveling style, as x and y axis criteria.

After, I wrote down three major tasks that such types of people would do when they need my service. Then, I decided to choose which types of users I should focus to define my target users. Then, based on the previous key findings and target users, I created personas, persona scenario, and journey maps to identify my target users & users’ problems.

Process: Develop

In develop phase, I should consider WHAT to deliver, a service blueprint. To do this, previous research team members gathered for ideation sprint. Each member had 30 minutes of allotted time to come up with 50+ ideas which address users’ problems.

After exploring diverse ideas, I picked 5 ideas that I want to focus and conducted Funnel Vision . By doing this, I was able to converge and distill ideas to evaluate properly, and find the best matched solution to my target users. With the abovementioned process, I finalized service key features, service concept to deliver UX values.

Based on UX values, I started developing design concept by utilizing mindmap and mood board.

Process: Deliver

In Deliver phase, based on previous process, I created wireframes and had low-fi user testing session to create final wireframe. Combining with all precious feedbacks, I finalized design and completed prototyping.
