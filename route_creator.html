Route Creator

An application that allows you
to make your own routes in the most convenient way

Type Individual Project
Role UX/UI Designer
Duration Summer 2018
Service Slogan Create your own routes in less than a minute. It’s super quick, flexible, and easy.

Overview

I am an enthusiastic traveler, but making an itinerary takes a lot of time and effort even for me.
Of course, Google Map is my savior in this, but I still often need extra papers to write down notes to schedule my trip. Whenever my plan undergoes some changes, I go back to the map, search places again, and compare distances to find the most efficient routes for my entire schedule.

I was given this project when taking full-time UX school class at Fast Campus. Fast Campus UX School is a competitive UX professional program in Korea, similar to General Assembly. Through the design process, I quickly noticed many users experience the same issues as I do. More importantly, no satisfactory mobile application helps users create their own travel routes, despite abundant need.

Based on rigorous research with insightful analysis, I finally designed an application, Route Creator. The service was nominated 3rd place among 23 others in in-class final design competition.

Goals

Route Creator helps users save time and effort when they want to:


1) quickly make their individualized and unique travel plans
2) flexibly adjust or change their trip schedules while traveling
3) easily find the most efficient routes for their everyday life

Approach

I utilized the double-diamond tactic for this project: Discover, Define, Develop, and Deliver.

In Discover phase, the project was conducted by a team, who were also working with the same topic, travel. We collaborately conducted quantitative and qualitative research including survey and individual in-depth interviews. Before delving into the next phase, I used an affinity diagram to organize ideas and data to draw key findings.

In Define phase, I defined purpose of design - what, how, and for whom - by identifying target users & problems through user segmentation, persona scenario, and a journey map.

In Develop phase, I created a blueprint to solve my users’ problems by incorporating creative ideation, UX strategy. Based on UX strategy, I started developing a design concept by utilizing mind maps, concept keywords, and a mood board.

In Deliver phase, I created wireframes and had a low-fi wireframe session, which provided me precious feedback from users. Finally, I finalized my final design and completed prototyping.

Process: Discover

In Discover phase, tailored research is key. As a team-based research, we conducted desk research by assigning each other different foci: domestic trend, global trend, or current popular travel app services.

After discussion of the outcomes, we conducted a survey with 100+ participants, and individual interviews to figure out current users' experiences with other travel apps and problems they encountered. We later, selected 4 interviewees, who gave insightful responses for in-depth individual interviews.

Key Findings

Process: Define

To me, Define is the most important phase, requiring more time and effort than any other phase. Defining WHO (target user), WHAT (service blueprint), HOW (method) is the heart of problem-solving.

As for the first step, I asked myself, “Who is my target user?” to identify my core users. In this process, I grabbed 5 sticky notes and define 5 types of people who may need my service the most. Based on this, I drew a user segmentation matrix based on two most important factors, age and traveling style, as x and y axis criteria.

After, I wrote down three major tasks that such types of people would perform when they need my service. Then, I decided to choose which types of users I should focus on to define my target users. Based on the above-mentioned key findings and target users, I created personas, persona scenario, and journey maps to identify my target users and users’ problems.

Process: Develop

In Develop phase, I should consider WHAT to deliver, a service blueprint . To do this, previous research team members gathered for an ideation sprint. Each member had 30 minutes of allotted time to come up with 50+ ideas to address users’ problems.

After exploring diverse ideas, I picked 5 ideas that I want to focus on and conducted Funnel Vision . By doing this, I was able to converge and distill ideas to evaluate them properly, and find the solution that best matched my target users. With the above-mentioned process, I finalized service key features, service concept to deliver UX values.

Based on UX values, I started developing a design concept by utilizing mindmaps and a mood board.

Process: Deliver

In Deliver phase, based on the previous processes, I created wireframes and had a low-fi user testing session to create final wireframe. Combining all precious feedbacks, I finalized design and completed prototyping.

Final Thoughts

This project gave me two important takeaways.

One: Although the market is red ocean, opportunities exist.

When I was initially given the project, I worried that the great number of travel apps available might make it hard for me to come up with fresh ideas. However, I’ve found that there are always needs untapped, which means tailored research along with keen insights can always reveal opportunities.

Two: Feedbacks make everything “so much” better.

From UX research to design, I’ve received tons of feedback not only from users but also other designers or project managers. Users’ feedbacks are the core part of everything, helping me to fully empathize with their need so that I don't over-rely on my personal opinions. Other designers’ feedback allowed me to further consider details of my design based on professional UX and aesthetic views. Project managers’ feedback allowed me to consider the business value of my product.
