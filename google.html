Google Design Challenge

Design an optimal experience for incoming students
to fully enjoy student organizations at University.

Type Individual Project
Role UX Designer
Tools Sketch, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Protopie
Timespan Spring 2019 | 1 week

Overview

During my internship recruiting process, I had an opportunity interviewing with Google. Sadly, by the time when I heard that they wanted to move forward to the next step, I already accepted my other offer so had to turn down the opportunity.

Regardless of the fact, this one-week design challenge was still very interesting and exciting. The challenge I had was:

Design an experience for new students to browse, search, and propose new student organizations.

Design Process

As I had only a week to complete the project, I set a clear design process to elicit the best outcome as follow:

Discover: User Research

After setting up the process, I started conducting research and discovering valuable ideas and insights through survey, in-depth interviews, and competitive analysis.

Define: Target Users and Problem Statement

With insightful findings from the previous stage, I defined target users and their needs through a persona and wrote the clear problem statement.

Develop: The Solution

By having a clear understanding of the core users and problem statement,now it's time for me to develop solutions. I did this through ideation sprint, user flow chart, lo/hi-fidelity wireframes. During the process, I constantly incorporated feedback to finalize service features.

Final Design

Why this solution?

1) The solution allows users to easily explore student organizations based on their personal interest. This would satisfy the majority of users’ (75%) need for “I want to explore student organizations heavily based on my personal interest.”

2) It provides one-stop mobile platfrom solution for students who want to browse, search, and propose student organizations, which currently does not exist despite abundant need.

3) Users would easily find weekly events hosted by student organizations through various filters and join the events through automatic-Google calendar integration. This would satisfy users’ need for “keeping informed of weekly events by student organizations”, facilitate students’ participation and spark general interest toward student organizations.

4) Direct message/email features would allow users to easily and more actively reach out to organizations, and it would satify users’ need for “having direct communication methods with student organizations.
